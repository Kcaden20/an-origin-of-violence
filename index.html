An Origin of Violence

By Kevin Cadena

I. THEY CALL IT A CIVIL WAR

They called it a Civil War fought by conservatives and liberals.

Yet with the state supporting tradition and killing progress, it was more a massacre.

They say it originated with the assassination of Jorge Eliecer Gaitan Ayala, the liberal presidential candidate.

Riots of rage sparked from the salt of tears as a man who killed a man was killed by men.

How could the violence reach the city? With its shining white concrete meant to stay pure.

"This was only meant for over there!"" They might've said. Believing red should only compliment shades of green; natural or otherwise.

All for our good neighbor's sweet yellow cravings.

They deserved this. What was the state supposed to do besides choose a side?

Never mind the 18 years of violence prior in the countryside

Never mind the conservatives coming to power two years prior.

Never mind their leaders who spoke of law and order yet called to take the land back by any means necessary.

That it was theirs.

That the other side was the enemy.

The land has always been the land.

II. THEY CALL IT AN ORIGIN STORY

What does it mean to have an origin when all the pieces matter?

Is it deciding one part matters over the others? Didn't they all come together to create the full picture of our being?

La Violencia happened half a century before I was born.

Its pot had simmered for almost 25 years before that.

The formation of a nation-state 100 years before that.

The forcing of the few who'd survived first contact to lose their identity and farm the battleground for half a millenia before that.

Those few included a few of my ancestors, who prior to assimilation had inhabited the land for so long that their origin story was the origin of the earth.

The land has always been the land.

III.I. THEY CALL IT KINSHIP

My mom's mom grew up hearing about it.

Learning about it through her mom's face and hands, full of anger and irritation.

Hopefully the mountains would protect them.

Hopefully a large family would protect them.

Hopefully a community would protect them.

They wouldn't come back to see a blue cross on their door.

The mountains would hold its promise for 45 years. That was all they could do.

At the turn of the century, their body shook and took down their hard work.

The land has always been the land.

III.II. THEY CALL IT AUTONOMY

My father's father grew up hearing about it.

Learning about it through his father's face and hands, full of anger and humiliation.

Hopefully the mountains would protect them.

Hopefully owning a business would protect them.

Hopefully being self-reliant would protect them.

The mountains would hold its promise for 45 years. That was all they could do.

At the turn of the century, their body shook but spared their hard work.

The land has always been the land.

IV. THEY CALL IT A FAMILY

The event's effects were not felt.

The event's effect's effects were.

Claudia growing up with her mom, grandparents and great-grandparents. A show of community.

Jose growing up with his dad raising another family. A test of self-reliance.

Their place in the mountains was small enough, they could talk to each other from across their barrios.

From elementary to high school.

From alone to together.

From alone together to a family.

From their city in the mountains to the city by the rivers.

The land has always been the land.

V. THEY CALL IT AN ORIGIN OF VIOLENCE

My mom's mom's origin came after her family had escaped.

My dad's dad's origin came after his family had escaped.

My parents' origins only came after their families had escaped.

My origins only came after my family had escaped.

The land has always been the land.
